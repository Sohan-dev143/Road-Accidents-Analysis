# Road Accident Data Analysis 
## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing 12,000+ road accident records to identify key factors influencing accident frequency and severity. The analysis covers driver behavior, vehicle types, environmental conditions, and time-based patterns to generate actionable insights.

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains 18 variables, including:
* Driver demographics (age, gender, experience)
* Vehicle type
* Road surface condition
* Weather condition
* Accident cause
* Accident severity
* Time and location of accident

## Key Insights
### Accident Severity
* 84.5% accidents resulted in slight injuries
* Only 1.3% were fatal accidents
### Major Causes
* Lane changing → highest contributor
* Lack of safe distance → second major cause
* Overspeeding → major factor in severe accidents
### Time Patterns
* Most accidents occur during PM hours
* Weekdays show higher accident frequency
### Vehicle Analysis
* Automobiles and heavy vehicles dominate accidents
Heavy vehicles are linked to higher severity
### Environmental Impact
* Most accidents occur in normal weather conditions
* Weather has less impact compared to human factors

## Key Learnings  
* Driver behavior is the leading cause of road accidents.
* Accident frequency and severity are not always directly related.
* Evening hours show the highest accident occurrence.
* Urban areas have frequent but low-severity accidents.
* Heavy vehicles are associated with higher accident severity.

## Recommendations
* Enforce strict lane discipline and safe driving practices.
* Implement strong speed control and monitoring systems.
* Improve traffic management during peak hours.
* Regulate heavy vehicle movement in high-traffic areas.
* Conduct awareness programs for safe driving behavior.
* Enhance road infrastructure and junction design.
* Use smart surveillance for real-time traffic monitoring.
